Sea Turtle

Sand temperature determines the sex of sea turtle hatchlings. Warm nests produce mostly females, and cold nests produce mostly males.

Sea turtles are long-lived marine reptiles with streamlined shells and strong flippers, roaming warm oceans for thousands of miles. They surface to breathe, feed on seagrass, algae, or jellyfish, and females return to sandy beaches to lay eggs, repeating a life cycle millions of years old.
